Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: What are Bana Bags made of?

+
Bana Bags are made from recycled waste paper and old cardboard cartons. These materials are cleaned, processed, and turned into strong seedling bags. They don’t contain plastic, chemicals, or anything harmful. Once planted, the bags start breaking down slowly and add nutrients back into the soil.

Q2: Are Bana Bags safe for all crops?

+
Yes. Farmers can use our seed bags for vegetables, fruits, herbs, and other plants. We’ve tested them with crops like tomatoes, onions, and green peppers. The bags hold soil and water well during the early growth stages. When you plant the whole bag into the ground, it breaks down naturally, which means less damage to young roots and better growth.

Q3: How long do Bana Bags last before they start breaking down?

+
Our bags are strong enough to last through seedling growth, usually 3 to 6 weeks depending on the crop. Once they’re planted into the soil, they start to break down slowly over 2–3 months. They turn into organic matter that helps feed the soil. This means less cleanup for farmers and better soil quality over time.

Q4: How can I place an order or get more information?

+
Right now, you can contact us through phone, email, or visit one of our partner centers. We’re also launching a mobile app soon where farmers can order, ask questions, and track their deliveries. If you're part of a cooperative, we can arrange bulk delivery directly to your location.

Q5: Can I buy Bana Bags in bulk?

+
Yes. We supply both small and large orders. If you’re a cooperative, greenhouse, or large farm, we offer bulk pricing and delivery options. We also give simple training on how to store, use, and plant with Bana Bags to make sure you get the best results.

Q6: How do Bana Bags help with soil health and climate change?

+
Unlike plastic bags, Bana Bags break down naturally and don’t leave harmful waste. When they decompose, they add organic matter to the soil. This helps the soil hold more water, improves structure, and reduces the need for chemical fertilizers. Using our bags is one way farmers can fight climate change while growing strong crops.

Q7: Are Bana Bags approved by the government or other organizations?

+
Yes. Our mission supports Rwanda’s environmental goals and national policies against plastic use. We’re also working with local NGOs and partners like the Rwanda Green Fund to make sure our products meet safety and sustainability standards.
